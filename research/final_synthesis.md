## The Investment Framework

### Primary Thesis: Liquid Cooling Technology

**The Core Bet:**
AI infrastructure requires liquid cooling. Period.

- NVIDIA GB200 NVL72: 120kW/rack - liquid cooling mandatory
- Next-gen targeting 500kW/rack
- Air cooling ceiling: ~40kW/rack
- The gap cannot be bridged by operational optimization

**Market Size:**
- 2025: $5-6B
- 2030: $15-20B
- 2035: $25-45B
- CAGR: 20-28%

### Technology Preference Within Liquid Cooling

| Technology | Best For | Limitations | Investment Grade |
|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Two-Phase DTC | Extreme density (150-500kW) | PFAS regulatory risk | A |
| Single-Phase DTC | Moderate-high density (50-150kW) | Lower ceiling | A- |
| Two-Phase Immersion | HPC, crypto mining | Operational complexity | B+ |
| Single-Phase Immersion | HPC, specialized | Tank-based constraints | B |

**Recommendation:** Diversified exposure to liquid cooling with overweight on two-phase DTC.

### Secondary Thesis: Geographic Arbitrage

**The Nordic Opportunity:**
- Natural climate enables year-round free cooling
- 100% renewable energy (hydro, geothermal)
- PUE <1.10 achievable without chillers
- atNorth acquisition at $4B validates thesis

**Investment Vehicles:**
- Nordic DC operators (public or PE-backed)
- Infrastructure funds with Nordic exposure
- Renewable energy + DC location plays

### Avoid: Speculative Technology

**No Investment in:**
- Radiative cooling for DCs (physics don't work)
- Novel cooling approaches without hyperscaler validation
- Air cooling optimization (declining market)

## Risk Assessment

### Primary Risks to Thesis A (Two-Phase)

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| PFAS regulations expand | 30% | High | HFO alternatives exist |
| Single-phase catches up | 35% | Medium | Diversify across liquid types |
| GPU TDP plateaus | 20% | Medium | Still need liquid for current density |
| Supply chain concentration | 25% | Medium | Multiple players scaling |

**Net Assessment:** Manageable risks with clear mitigation paths.

### Why Thesis B Isn't an Investment Thesis

High ambient tolerance is valuable but not investable:
1. It's an operational practice, not a technology
2. You can't buy equity in "running data centers at higher temperatures"
3. Hyperscalers already do this - no growth opportunity
4. Doesn't solve the density problem that requires liquid cooling

### Why Thesis C Should Be Avoided

Radiative cooling for data centers is not viable:
1. Scale mismatch: 100 W/m² vs 100 kW/rack
2. Geographic constraints: Fails in humid climates where DCs are built
3. No path to improvement: Physics limits, not engineering
4. SkyCool is a building HVAC company, not DC cooling

## Signposts to Monitor

### Bullish Signals (Increase Allocation)

1. **Hyperscaler liquid cooling % disclosure** - If Meta/Google/Microsoft report >50% liquid-cooled AI capacity
2. **New two-phase fluid approved** - Non-PFAS fluid with equivalent performance
3. **GPU TDP exceeds 2kW** - Forces remaining air-cooled operators to convert
4. **M&A activity** - More acquisitions at premium valuations

### Bearish Signals (Reduce Allocation)

1. **PFAS ban includes data centers** - Would disrupt two-phase specifically
2. **Single-phase breakthrough** - 20,000+ W/m²·K achieved in production
3. **GPU efficiency breakthrough** - TDP drops despite compute increase
4. **Hyperscaler insources cooling** - Build vs buy shift

### Neutral but Notable

1. **Orbital computing growth** - Interesting for technology development, minimal DC impact
2. **Nordic DC construction boom** - Validates high ambient thesis but not investable as cooling
3. **Water scarcity acceleration** - Benefits closed-loop liquid over evaporative
