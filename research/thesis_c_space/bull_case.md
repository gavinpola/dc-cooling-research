# Bull Case: Space-Based Passive Radiative Cooling Has Potential

**Thesis:** Passive radiative cooling to space represents a revolutionary approach that could meaningfully contribute to data center cooling, especially as the technology matures.

**Date:** April 13, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

While radiative cooling cannot single-handedly solve data center cooling challenges today, the bulls argue it represents a significant opportunity for several reasons:

1. **Zero electricity consumption** - True passive cooling
2. **Zero water consumption** - Critical for sustainability
3. **Complementary to existing systems** - 10-40% efficiency gains
4. **Growing edge computing market** - Lower-density applications
5. **Early-stage technology** - Room for dramatic improvement

The thesis is not that radiative cooling replaces liquid cooling, but that it becomes a valuable layer in the cooling stack.

## 1. The Physics Are Real and Compelling

### Proven Science, Not Speculation

**The atmospheric window exists:**
- 8-13 μm transparency to space is physics, not theory
- Space is a near-infinite heat sink at 3K
- The mechanism has been demonstrated repeatedly at Stanford, UC Davis, and commercial deployments

**Stanford Demonstration:**
- 97% solar reflectivity achieved
- Sub-ambient cooling during daytime proven
- Technology works - the question is scale, not viability

### Performance Is Already Useful

**Achieved cooling powers:**
- 100-150 W/m² theoretical maximum
- 93 W/m² demonstrated in production coatings
- SkyCool panels delivering 10-40% efficiency improvements

**In Context:**
- 100 W/m² across a warehouse roof is meaningful
- 10% reduction in HVAC energy for a data center building is real savings
- This technology exists and works TODAY

## 2. The Zero-Water, Zero-Electricity Value Proposition

### Water Scarcity Is Accelerating

**Data center water consumption:**
- Evaporative cooling uses billions of gallons annually
- Microsoft committed to 95% water reduction by 2024
- Water scarcity is limiting data center locations

**Radiative Cooling Advantage:**
- Zero water consumption
- Works in water-scarce regions where evaporative fails
- Aligns with sustainability commitments

### True Passive Operation

**Unlike ALL other cooling technologies:**
- No compressors
- No pumps
- No fans required at the panel level
- No electricity consumption for heat rejection

**HoMEDUCS Project Achievement:**
> "Integrating Skycool panels with polymer heat exchangers and cold plates can bring cooling energy consumption down to less than 5% of total power."

This is approaching theoretical PUE limits through passive means.

## 3. Complementary Integration Strategy

### Not Replacement, Augmentation

**The realistic bull case is NOT:**
- "Radiative cooling replaces liquid cooling"
- "Radiative cooling cools 100kW racks directly"

**The realistic bull case IS:**
- Radiative cooling handles building envelope loads
- Reduces base cooling requirements by 20-40%
- Allows active cooling to focus on IT equipment

### Stack Model

**Data Center Cooling Stack (Bull Vision):**

| Layer | Technology | Load |
|-------|------------|------|
| Chip-level | Direct-to-chip liquid | 60-70% of heat |
| Rack-level | CDU heat rejection | Moved to outdoor |
| Building | Radiative + minimal HVAC | Envelope + buffer |

**Result:** PUE approaching 1.02-1.05 through combined approach

### SkyCool Commercial Results

**Proven integration benefits:**
- Add-on to existing HVAC: 10-40% efficiency improvement
- Deployed across 6 US states
- Commercial warehouses seeing 21-65% cooling energy savings

## 4. Edge Computing Is The Real Opportunity

### Edge Deployments Have Different Requirements

**Edge computing characteristics:**
- Small scale (single racks, micro data centers)
- Distributed locations (often remote)
- Power-constrained sites
- Water unavailable in many locations

**Radiative Cooling Fit:**
- A 10 kW edge unit needs ~100 m² of panels
- Rooftop of small building can accommodate
- No water infrastructure required
- No electricity beyond minimal pumping

### The Math Works at Edge Scale

**Example: 10kW Edge Pod**
- Requirement: 10 kW cooling
- Radiative capacity @ 100 W/m²: 100 m²
- A 10m × 10m roof area
- Achievable for small facility

**Modular Data Centers:**
UC Davis demonstrated this integration works for modular setups in water-scarce regions.
